Rose Among Locusts

Volume I: The Shadow Over Aurelia

Scroll to begin the chronicle

The halls of the High Administratum always carried a specific scent: ancient parchment dust, heavy incense, and aged amasec. Aurelia von Wald had grown accustomed to this atmosphere. It belonged to those who held the quill while others held the sword. The Governor was muttering again about promethium quotas, his voice drowning in the mechanical hum of the life-support throne keeping his decrepit body functional.

His fingers tremble as he signs the edicts. The old fool doesn't even notice when I alter the figures in the reports. He is the past of this planet; I am its inevitable future.

Aurelia's stride was the metronome to which the Spire lived. The sharp click of her boots against the marble echoed through the high vaulted ceilings, forcing minor clerks to press themselves against the walls as she passed. Her guards followed behind—heavy, iron-clad silhouettes that served only as a backdrop to her refined majesty.

These soldiers... they are like furniture. Sturdy, reliable, and utterly hollow. One cannot simply possess power; one must demonstrate it with every rustle of a cloak.

Rare visits to the Lower Hive were a necessary evil. Aurelia knew that to keep the masses obedient, one must occasionally offer them the illusion that the high caste is aware of their existence. She looked at a soot-covered child without a shred of pity—only a professional curiosity about how humans could survive in such an all-encompassing stench.

My hands are gloved, yet I can still feel the oily grime in the air. Look at me, little creature. To you, I am a goddess descended from the Spire.

Officer Kastiel was useful. His loyalty to the Imperium was unwavering, but his loyalty to Aurelia was a matter of artistry. She skillfully wove a web around the military command, using her charm as precisely as an executioner uses an axe. A quiet office, tactical maps, and the faint smell of gun oil—the perfect setting for intrigue.

He thinks we are partners. In truth, I only require the access codes to the orbital platforms. His devotion is a key I turn whenever it suits me.

Dress uniforms, banners fluttering in the wind, and thousands of hobnailed boots striking the plastisteel. For many, war was a tragedy; for Aurelia, it was the highest form of logistics. Moving regiments, allocating resources, striking out names—it provided a sense of true, divine dominion.

I do not see people; I see resources. It is like assembling a puzzle where the pieces occasionally die, but the overall picture remains flawless. Some say war is hell. To me, it is mathematics.

Night brought a silence broken only by the crackle of candles and the low hum of servo-skulls sorting archives. Aurelia allowed herself a brief moment of weakness—sleep at her desk amidst the paperwork. She was confident in tomorrow, for the world she had built seemed eternal.

Another successful cycle. Taxes collected, opposition suppressed. The stars are so quiet tonight. Nothing can disturb this harmony... or so I thought.

The air suddenly turned sweet and sticky. Then came the Shadow. Communication died, vox-channels filled with animalistic screeching. Aurelia stood by the Spire's window, watching as the sky of her world turned into a vortex of living flesh. The Tyranids weren't just attacking—they were consuming reality itself.

This is no fleet... it is an organism. It is eating the sky. My order... my reports... none of it matters anymore. I will not be digested.

The Spire trembled under the impact of bio-spores. Former elegance was forgotten. Aurelia clawed through the rubble, her luxurious garments reduced to rags, her fingers beaten and bloody. Chaos reigned: aristocrats who once quarreled over dinner seating were now dying in the jaws of Gargoyles.

Run. Do not look back. Kastiel died or perhaps he simply fled—it doesn't matter. All that matters is that I am still breathing. I will find a way out. I always do.

When the first creature lunged at her, Aurelia didn't scream. She felt a feral, primal rage. The dagger that was once a mere ornament on her belt sank deep into the soft flesh between chitinous plates. Green ichor splattered her face, burning her skin, but she only gripped the hilt tighter.

You thought I was but a fragile flower? Taste this, abomination! I am Aurelia von Wald, and I claim your life to save my own!

The city burned. Promethium fires illuminated the silhouettes of Tyranid bio-ships descending further into the atmosphere. Aurelia stood amidst the ashes of her former life, clutching a pistol. She had lost everything: her title, her wealth, her army of servants. But she kept what truly made her dangerous—her ambition and her will.

The planet is doomed. Но somewhere beyond the veil of smoke, the shuttles are still waiting. I will escape this place. The Rose didn't wither... it was tempered in fire.
