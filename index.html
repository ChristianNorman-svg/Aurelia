Volume I: The Shadow Over Aurelia

Colored text indicates psychological strategy

The halls of the High Administratum always carried a specific scent: ancient parchment dust, heavy incense, and aged amasec. Aurelia von Wald, a high-born Questor, watched the fading Governor through a veil of professional indifference.

His fingers tremble as he signs the edicts. He is the past of this planet; I am its inevitable future. My blood grants me status, but my mind grants me control.

The sharp click of her boots against the marble echoed through the high vaulted ceilings, forcing minor clerks to press themselves against the walls as she passed.

These soldiers are but furniture, framing my silhouette. One cannot simply possess power; one must demonstrate it with every rustle of a cloak.

Rare visits to the Lower Hive were a necessary evil to keep the masses obedient. She offered them a practiced, cold smile.

Remember this image, little creature. To you, I am a goddess descended from the Spire. This mercy costs me but a single smile, yet it buys another week without riots.

Officer Kastiel was useful. She skillfully wove a web around the military command, using her charm as precisely as an executioner uses an axe.

He thinks we are partners. In truth, his devotion is a key I turn whenever it suits me. I will have those orbital platform codes by tonight.

For many, war was a tragedy; for Questor Aurelia, it was the highest form of logistics. Moving regiments was like moving ink across a page.

I do not see people; I see resources. It is like assembling a puzzle where the pieces occasionally die, but the overall picture remains flawless. War is mathematics.

Night brought a silence broken only by the crackle of candles. Aurelia allowed herself a brief moment of sleep at her desk.

The stars are so quiet tonight. Taxes collected, opposition suppressed. Nothing can disturb this harmony... or so I thought.

The rot did not begin with a roar. As a Questor, Aurelia had known of their existence from the glaring discrepancies in the tithe records.

I found them first in the ledgers—a shadow hidden in the math. I tracked them through percentages and logistics... and now, it seems, they have tracked me.

The air suddenly turned sweet and sticky. Communication died. The sky of her world turned into a vortex of living flesh.

It is eating the sky. My order... my reports... none of it matters anymore. But I will not be digested. I am a von Wald.

The elevator doors hissed open to a nightmare. The Prefect stumbled as the pale, four-armed shadows reached out from the dark.

He pleads for a hand I will not give. In the cold arithmetic of survival, a dead weight is a death sentence. His screams will provide the distraction I need. Farewell, dear prefect.

She shattered the ornate transparisteel window and leapt into the smog-choked air. Behind her, the cultists didn't shoot to kill; they pursued with a feral, desperate hunger. They sought not her death, but her infection—to add her noble blood to their twisted communion.

They don't want my life; they want my genes. The Genestealer's Kiss... a fate worse than death. I would rather shatter every bone in my body on the pavement below than become a brood mother for their abomination.

Aurelia clawed through the rubble, her luxurious garments reduced to rags. High-born and low-born alike died in the jaws of the swarm.

Run. Do not look back. Kastiel is dead weight now. All that matters is that I am still breathing. I will find a way out. I always do.

The dagger that was once a mere ornament sank deep into chitinous flesh. Green ichor splattered her face, burning her skin.

You thought I was but a fragile flower? Taste this, abomination! I claim your life to save my own!

The city burned. Questor Aurelia stood amidst the ashes, clutching a pistol. She had lost her title, but she kept her will.

The planet is doomed, but I am not. The Rose didn't wither... it was tempered in fire.
